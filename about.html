About This Project

Authors: Lauren Sampson, Jing Li, Yujia Shi

This project is inspired by the sheer universality of death. Despite death being a fate that everyone eventually shares, the way people die can provide a lot of insight about their lives—specifically their socio-economic status and location they lived in. Our goal for this website is to tell a story of the rate at which people die in different places and why that is. Hopefully, we can help show which countries need to work on various elements of their citizens lifestyles that might in fact contribute to the death factor.

The data we found is supported by Kaggle and is representative of 228 entities or locations and 28 different death factors. We decided to split them up based on similarity in factor (e.g. we have a bubble that breaks down the different environmental causes of death). By illustrating all of these factors in various visualizations, we hope to show users in many different formats, what the leading cause of death is in various locations.

Users should be able to select the death factor they want to look at and view visualizations as they scroll accordingly. First they will encounter a choropleth world map that operates where the color gradient correlates to the number of deaths. Then the user will be able to see a bar chart the numerically displays the top ten countries that contribute to that factor’s rate. The scatter plot indicates a correlation between the continents GDP and death rates — we wanted users to be able to associate the socio-economic factors that go into a locations either high or low death rates.

Overall, we wanted to make this a story about death rates and how they affect everyone on earth's lives. We looked at the ways location plays into death rate as well as GDP and overall continental averages. We hope you enjoy the website and feel free to reach out with any further questions.
